Business Law

New businesses face daunting legal questions. How should the business be organized? How can it mitigate liability risks? How does taxation work? What registrations and licenses are needed? What contracts, leases, and disclosures are appropriate? Two Rivers Law is ready to help you launch your operation, from the cutting edge business to the family firm to the low-key, part-time consulting gig. We tailor our services to your budget and willingness to tackle tasks on your own, so you pay for only the assistance that you need and want.

Business Law

Expanding a business can be challenging. Two Rivers Law can help you plan your business strategy and transactions in light of tax, intellectual property, and regulatory considerations. Whether you are hiring your first employee, bringing in new business partners, or expanding over state lines, we can help.

Mission-Driven Enterprise

We love assisting businesses that strive to meet multiple bottom lines, such as cooperatives and other mission-driven business forms. While the possibilities for socially responsive business are exciting, the regulatory environment (including taxation, employment, financing) has not caught up. And because some laws are so new, there can be uncertainty about what they mean. Two Rivers Law can steer you through the regulations to create a stable, forward-looking, mission-driven business.

Nonprofit Law

We absolutely love working with nonprofits, from scrappy start-ups to established, multi-faceted service organizations. We have experience with diverse nonprofits, including scientific, artistic, and educational organizations as well as those directly serving the poor or disadvantaged. Whether you need help with a fiscal sponsorship, spinning off from another nonprofit, drafting a business document, Board training, starting a new program, closing a deal, or filing a 501(c) application, we can help.

Shoestring Budgets

WeRunning a nonprofit can be hard work. Funds are often tight and it can be difficult to figure out where limited resources should be directed. From flat-fee billing to sliding-scale hourly rates, our financial arrangements will not break your budget. We do not oversell you on legal services. If something is straightforward for you to do, we can show you how. Often we find that a 30- or 60-minute one-on-one educational overview of a particular area of law is all that is needed. There are excellent firms that help nonprofits, and some offer reduced rates to charitable nonprofits. If your matter can be handled faster or at lower cost by another firm, we will make appropriate referrals.

The New 1023-EZ

In September the IRS announced a new streamlined 501(c)(3) application procedure for many small nonprofits. For example, nonprofits that expect to bring in less than $50,000 a year often qualify for this easier application. The simpler application is a wonderful development. Rather than hiring specialists to help them with the cumbersome, ordinary application, many small organizations can take on the task themselves.

Sharing Law

What Is the Sharing Economy?

The term "Sharing Economy" means different things to different people. For some it means using technology ("apps") to crowdsource short-term rental arrangements. For example, many people think of using their smart phones to rent a room in someone's house, rent a car, or even "rent" a passenger seat in a car. The idea is to reduce the need to buy stuff—and so reduce overconsumption—by "sharing" stuff that people already own.

Getting Legal Help

Successful sharing arrangements need good planning. They can baffle regulators and fall into gray areas of the law. While running afoul of applicable rules can be disastrous, community-spirited programs also stumble over unanticipated sources of conflict—even among well-meaning and good-natured people. Two Rivers Law can help you engineer sound and peaceful solutions. Our flexible and sliding-scale billing options can put your sharing solution on a firm footing without killing your budget.
